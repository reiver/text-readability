Text Readability

Text Readability refers to how easily and comfortably human readers — including those with visual or cognitive impairments — can perceive, track, and consume written content based on typographic choices like font, contrast, and spacing.

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux (reiver)

Text Readability is how easy and comfortable it is for humans to read words on a screen or page or anywhere else where the written word appers.

Some text is difficult to read — not because the words are difficult, but because the way the text is presented. It's affected by things most people never consciously notice. Things that affect text readability — that affect whether text feels easy to read or is difficult to read — includes:

  • the choice of font,
  • the size of the text,
  • the length of a line of text,
  • the text color and background color,
  • the constrast between text color and background color,
  • how much spacing sits between lines and paragraphs,
  • etc.

When these are done well, you read effortlessly and focus on what the words are saying. When they're done poorly your eyes tire, you lose your place, and you understand less of what you read.

When lines stretch too wide, the text becomes difficult to read. When text is too small, the text becomes difficult to read. When the contrast between the text color and the background color is either blinding or too faint, the text becomes difficult to read. When the spacing feels cramped, the text becomes difficult to read. Et.

Good text readability means the design disappears and only the content remains.
