Text Readability

Text readability refers to how easily and comfortably human readers — including those with visual or cognitive impairments — can perceive, track, and consume written content based on typographic choices like font, contrast, and spacing.

by Charles Iliya Krempeaux (reiver)

Some text is difficult to read — not because the words are difficult, but because the way the text is presented. The choice of font, the size of th text, the constrast betwen text color and background color, the spacing between lines and paragraph, etc, has a huge impact on whether text feels easy to read or is difficult to read. When lines stretch too wide, the text becomes difficult to read. When fonts are too small, the text becomes difficult to read. When the contrast between the text color and the background color is either blinding or too faint, the text becomes difficult to read. When the spacing feels cramped, the text becomes difficult to read.
