The Problem With Justified Text

Justified text (aligned on both the left and right edges) creates a book-like appearance — but, creates serious problems on the Web.

Unless sophisticated hyphenation is used (which on the Web it isn't), justified text creates uneven gaps between words — sometimes called “rivers of white” or “rivers of white space” that flow vertically through paragraphs.

These "rivers of white space" are distracting for all readers and devastating for dyslexic readers, for whom they continuously pull focus away from word shapes, drastically accelerating eye strain and dyslexia reading fatigue .

Text Alignment affects Text Readability. There are 4 common types of text alignment:

  • left
  • right
  • center
  • justify
